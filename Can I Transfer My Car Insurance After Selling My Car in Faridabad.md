## Why Is Car Insurance Transfer Important?

Once ownership changes, the insurance policy should also reflect the new vehicle owner.

A policy transfer helps:

- Keep insurance records updated.
- Support hassle-free claim settlement.
- Reduce legal complications.
- Ensure the correct owner receives policy benefits.
- Maintain continuous insurance coverage.

Ignoring this step may create unnecessary issues if the vehicle is involved in an accident after the sale.

---

## When Should the Insurance Be Transferred?

The insurance transfer process should begin as soon as the vehicle ownership transfer starts.

Ideally, the following documents should be updated together:

- Registration Certificate (RC)
- Car Insurance Policy
- Sale Agreement
- Pollution Under Control (PUC) Certificate
- Identity Proof
- Address Proof

Completing these formalities together helps ensure a smooth ownership transition.

---

## Documents Usually Required

Although documentation may vary depending on the insurance company, the following are commonly requested:

## Can the Buyer Buy a New Insurance Policy Instead?

Yes.

Instead of transferring the existing policy, some buyers prefer purchasing a new insurance policy.

Common reasons include:

- Better premium options
- Improved coverage
- Additional add-on covers
- Policy renewal approaching
- Comparing multiple insurance providers

Choosing between policy transfer and a new policy depends on the buyer's insurance needs.
